HR Analytics Dashboard – Description

The HR Analytics Dashboard is an interactive business intelligence dashboard designed to help HR teams monitor workforce performance, employee demographics, and attrition trends. It provides a centralized view of key HR metrics, enabling data-driven decisions to improve employee retention, workforce planning, and organizational performance.

Dashboard Overview

At the top of the dashboard, key performance indicators (KPIs) provide a quick summary of the organization's workforce:

Total Employees: 1.48K employees in the organization.
Active Employees: 1.239K employees are currently active.
Average Age: 36.94 years.
Average Monthly Income: 49.51K.
Average Performance Rating: 3.15 out of 5.
Average Experience: 11.28 years.
Dashboard Visualizations
Department Filter: Users can filter the dashboard by departments such as Administration, Finance, Human Resources, IT, Marketing, Operations, and Sales for department-specific analysis.
Count of Attrition by Department: A donut chart displays employee attrition across departments. The Operations department records the highest attrition (540 employees – 36.49%), followed by Administration (425) and Sales (214).
Attrition by Salary Slab: A horizontal bar chart compares attrition across different salary ranges (0–3 LPA, 3–6 LPA, 6–10 LPA, and 10+ LPA), helping HR understand whether salary influences employee turnover.
Job Role & Job Satisfaction Matrix: This table shows job satisfaction ratings for different job roles, allowing HR to identify roles with lower satisfaction levels that may require attention.
Age Group Distribution: A column chart illustrates employee distribution across age groups. Most employees belong to the 26–35 and 36–45 age groups, indicating a predominantly mid-career workforce.
Attrition by Gender: A pie chart shows that Male employees account for 60% (888) of attrition, while Female employees account for 40% (592).
Attrition Trend by Experience: A line chart visualizes how attrition varies with years of experience, helping identify experience levels where employee turnover is highest.
Employee Count by Department: A funnel chart displays the number of employees in each department. Operations has the largest workforce, followed by Administration and Sales, while Finance has the smallest.
Business Insights

This dashboard enables HR managers and leadership teams to:

Monitor employee attrition and workforce trends.
Identify departments with high turnover.
Analyze the relationship between salary, experience, and attrition.
Understand workforce demographics by age and gender.
Evaluate employee satisfaction across job roles.
Support strategic decisions related to hiring, retention, compensation, and employee engagement.

Overall, the HR Analytics Dashboard provides a comprehensive view of an organization's human resources data, allowing stakeholders to make informed decisions that improve employee satisfaction, reduce attrition, and optimize workforce management.
